# Supervisor Coherence Report

## Scope

This review assesses the revised artefact against the validated source texts:

- `Cadre theorique_S1-S2-S3 artefact.md`
- `S1-S2-S3 Artefact - Content.md`

The assessment is deliberately framed with the rigor of a thesis supervisor: focus is placed on conceptual coherence, fidelity to the validated text base, and the defensibility of the framework in a doctoral setting.

## Overall Assessment

The revised artefact is now substantially stronger than the earlier version on three decisive points:

1. It is structurally aligned with the validated four-part architecture:
   `Why this research` / `Illustration` / `Theoretical framework` / `S1 / S2 / S3`.
2. It now reflects the core doctoral positioning validated in the source material:
   `P1 = what is reconfigured`, `P2 = the mechanism`, `P3 = the conditions`, with `managerial implication` treated as a derived implication rather than a fourth proposition.
3. It makes the S1/S2/S3 observational frame legible as an empirical protocol rather than as a normative prescription.

In supervisory terms: the artefact is now much closer to a defensible exploratory research framework, and much less like an overextended conceptual model.

## Strong Alignments

### 1. Research positioning is now coherent

The `Why this research` section accurately reflects the validated logic:

- consulting firms assume AI-augmented delivery will generate greater perceived value;
- yet in consulting, perceived value is co-created and only becomes real through client perception;
- therefore, operational gains do not automatically imply greater perceived value.

This is theoretically well aligned with the thesis positioning.

### 2. The research gap is properly framed

The artefact now correctly distinguishes:

- a **theoretical gap**:
  no existing framework synthesizes the constituent links of perceived value in consulting into a unified causal sequence, nor theorizes how AI reconfigures each link;
- a **practical gap**:
  business models assume more AI means greater perceived value for clients without theorizing the conditions under which this perception emerges.

This dual gap formulation is one of the strongest features of the revised artefact.

### 3. P1-P2-P3 form a coherent explanatory chain

The artefact now correctly treats:

- **P1** as the proposition about what is reconfigured,
- **P2** as the mediating mechanism,
- **P3** as the conditions under which that mechanism succeeds.

This is conceptually much stronger than treating P2 and P3 as parallel propositions at the same logical level.

### 4. S1/S2/S3 is correctly positioned as observational framework

The artefact now clearly presents S1/S2/S3 as:

- an empirical observation frame,
- not a normative model,
- not a substitute for the theoretical propositions.

This is exactly the right move in a thesis context.

## Residual Tensions To Keep Explicit

### 1. The P -> R feedback loop remains a proposed extension

The artefact correctly signals that the `P -> R` feedback loop is proposed.
That remains important.

Supervisory note:
this loop is plausible and interesting, but it is not grounded in the literature at the same level as the main `R -> P -> C` chain. In the thesis manuscript and viva, it should continue to be presented as a theoretically reasoned extension, not as an established causal law.

### 2. AI literacy remains an original extension, not an empirically settled mechanism in consulting

The artefact now justifies AI literacy well:

- theoretically original in AI-assisted consulting,
- methodologically tractable in a single exploratory case.

This is a strong justification.
However, the artefact should continue to avoid suggesting that the AI literacy -> attribution link is already firmly established in consulting-specific empirical literature. It is a thesis contribution, not a received truth.

### 3. Khan & Mishra (2024) must remain positioned as adjacent evidence

Their use in the artefact is acceptable and useful, provided the B2B caveat remains explicit:

- they support a convergent disclosure/credibility logic,
- but they come from adjacent consumer-AI settings,
- so the consulting-specific effect remains part of the thesis's originality claim.

This is defensible.
Overclaiming would not be.

### 4. The R/P/C dimension labels remain partly synthetic

The triad is now coherent and much clearer.
But it remains a synthetic framework rather than a direct reproduction of an existing one.

That is not a weakness.
It is a contribution.
Still, it should continue to be framed as such:

- conceptually grounded,
- literature-informed,
- but newly assembled for the AI-consulting context.

## UX / Scholarly Communication Assessment

The new interface is better aligned with doctoral communication needs:

- section-based reading flow instead of hidden modal panels,
- better thematic hierarchy,
- clearer distinction between positioning, theory, observation and illustration,
- more readable relation between propositions and empirical illustration.

The new R -> P -> C diagram is also stronger than the previous version because it no longer presents AI only as destabilizing. It now makes visible that AI can:

- amplify,
- destabilize,
- or invert

specific links depending on contingencies.

This is a substantial improvement in conceptual honesty.

## Remaining Recommendations

### Priority 1

In the thesis manuscript, keep stating explicitly that the artefact is exploratory and analytically generative, not statistically generalizable.

### Priority 2

When presenting P1 orally or in writing, keep the distinction very explicit:

- the `R -> P -> C` sequence is not the original contribution;
- the original contribution is the contingent AI reconfiguration of each link.

### Priority 3

Do not let the practical elegance of the artefact oversell empirical certainty.
Some formulations are persuasive and polished; in the thesis, they should remain tethered to the exploratory status of the research design.

## Final Supervisory Verdict

The revised artefact is now coherent with the validated source texts and much better aligned with a credible doctoral framing.

It is especially strong on:

- problem framing,
- proposition architecture,
- observational logic,
- and practitioner relevance.

The remaining task is not conceptual redesign, but continued vigilance on:

- level of claim,
- explicit treatment of exploratory status,
- and careful separation between literature-grounded mechanisms and thesis-specific theoretical extensions.
